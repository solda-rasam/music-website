rock

Rock music is a broad genre of popular music that originated in the late 1940s and early 1950s under the name "rock and roll" and expanded into a wide range of styles in the mid-1960s and later, especially in the United Kingdom and the United States. The genre has its roots in rock and roll of the 1940s and 1950s; a style that is directly derived from the blues and rhythm and blues genres of American slave music and from country music, and also incorporates influences from jazz and classical music. The name rock comes from the verb to rock in English, meaning to shake and sway. Common instruments in rock music include electric guitar, bass guitar, drums and keyboards, which are usually played in a band. Initially, rock was based on a 4/4 time signature and used a lot of beat-chorus songs, although over time rock music has become very diverse in terms of structure. Rock songs were mostly written for love and affection, but in the 1960s, civic and political issues also entered rock music. From the 1950s until the early 21st century, rock was the most popular musical style in the United States and much of the Western world.
